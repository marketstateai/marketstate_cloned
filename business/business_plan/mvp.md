## Working title

**MarketState MVP**  
Turn scattered investing into a goal-driven system that shows whether you are on track, why, and what to change.

---

## 1. Core beliefs

- Every person should own part of a good business.
- Long-term investing is hard because patience is hard, but it becomes more engaging when people can clearly visualize where they may end up in 5–10 years.
- People want to feel in control of their financial future.
- Historical behavior and historical portfolio data are extremely valuable and should influence future decisions.
- Most investment platforms are optimized for activity, noise, and product pushing, not for clear long-term progress.

---

## 2. The problem

Active retail investors often have:

- multiple brokers
- pensions
- crypto accounts
- taxable accounts
- partial views of their net worth
- weak performance tracking
- no real feedback loop tied to a long-term goal

As a result, they may know they are “investing,” but they often do **not** know:

- what they fully own across accounts
- what their true net worth is
- how they are performing versus their own goal
- how they are performing versus a benchmark
- whether their portfolio behavior is strategic or reactive
- what they should change now to improve future outcomes

Their current workaround is usually a mix of broker apps, rough mental models, and messy spreadsheets.

---

## 3. Target user for the MVP

### Primary user segment

Singles and couples without children, age **25–40**, with some disposable income, already investing, and motivated to grow their net worth intentionally.

### Initial geography

Start with **London**.  
Future expansion could include cities like New York, Los Angeles, Berlin, Tokyo, and Atlanta.

### Current behavior

This user already invests through platforms such as:

- Interactive Brokers
- Trading 212
- company pension platforms
- Coinbase
- Revolut
- similar low-cost retail brokerages

They often own 2+ accounts and may also hold crypto or pension assets.

### Why they feel the pain more than others

- They are active enough to feel fragmentation.
- They are thoughtful enough to care about goals.
- They are not rich enough to outsource everything to a traditional wealth manager.
- They are usually exposed to hype, trend-chasing, and product-led broker UX.
- They want to build wealth deliberately, not just “buy random winners.”

### Why they would adopt early

- They already feel the pain.
- They are willing to pay for clarity and control.
- A paid early-access or lifetime deal creates commitment, feedback, and bootstrap capital.
- They are likely to give useful product feedback and testimonials.

---

## 4. Core value proposition

### Main promise

**Turn scattered investing into a goal-driven system that shows whether you are on track, why, and what to change.**

### The user transformation

#### After 30 days

The user has:

- all major assets in one place
- a clear long-term financial target
- a realistic benchmark for what is achievable
- a high-level understanding of whether they are on track
- a simple action plan for where to focus next
- early insight into hidden drags such as fees, FX costs, poor performers, or overexposure

#### After 12 months

The user has:

- a year of structured history
- a record of goals versus actual outcomes
- a clear view of what worked and what did not
- stronger investing habits
- a better basis for next-year decision making

### Why current tools fall short

Most current tools do one piece of the job:

- brokers make investing easy, but push activity and noise
- spreadsheets provide control, but are manual and fragile
- portfolio trackers show holdings, but often lack a real behavioral loop
- financial advisors are expensive and reduce the user’s feeling of control

There is a gap for a product that combines:

- clarity
- education
- portfolio consolidation
- progress tracking
- behavioral feedback
- control

---

## 5. Product thesis

This is **not** just a portfolio tracker.

It is a **personal investing operating system** for people who want to:

1. define a meaningful long-term target  
2. see their complete current position  
3. understand whether they are on track  
4. review and improve their decisions over time

The emotional promise is just as important as the functional one:

> “You are in control, and you can see where your investing behavior is taking you.”

---

## 6. Smallest useful MVP

The MVP should be intentionally narrow. It only needs to solve the core promise for one focused user segment.

### Must-have 1: Goal setting and realistic benchmarking

The product should help users set a long-term investing goal with:

- target amount
- time horizon
- current capital
- expected monthly contributions
- benchmark assumptions
- simple education on likely ranges of outcomes

The app should also teach basic frameworks such as:

- long-term market return expectations
- trade-offs between passive investing and active investing
- fund manager pros and cons
- risks of trying to outperform the market
- the behavioral reality of patience, loss aversion, and hype

This does **not** need to become a giant education platform.  
It just needs enough education to help users set realistic expectations.

### Must-have 2: Consolidated portfolio overview

The user needs a simple, beautiful “source of truth” with two levels:

#### Level 1: high-level view
Answers:
- what do I own?
- what am I worth?
- am I on track versus my goal?
- am I ahead or behind?
- what is my progress over time?

#### Level 2: drill-down view
Answers:
- how is each holding performing?
- which positions are helping or hurting?
- what is concentrated?
- what has changed recently?
- what deserves review?

This should feel less like a broker and more like a reflection and control layer.

### Must-have 3: Connected market context

The app should not feel isolated from the market.

The MVP should include lightweight market context such as:

- major index performance
- sector performance
- stock-level snapshots
- exchange / broker / instrument availability information where possible

This helps users feel connected to what is happening beyond their own portfolio.

## 9. Core recurring loop

This is the engine that makes the product a business, not just a one-time dashboard.

### Trigger

The user is prompted to check in regularly, ideally weekly, and also whenever they:

- add new capital
- notice market moves
- want to review performance
- feel uncertainty about what to do next

### Core action

The user spends up to **30 minutes per week** doing one or more of the following:

- reviewing progress toward their long-term goal
- checking where performance is deviating from expectations
- journaling assumptions about the market
- reviewing current holdings
- identifying positions that no longer deserve capital
- deciding where new capital should go

### Insight / output

The app gives them:

- a clear on-track / off-track signal
- a benchmark comparison
- a simple explanation of why they are ahead or behind
- prompts to reflect on assumptions and decisions
- suggested areas for review, not just noise

### Behavior change

The user develops the habit of:

- reviewing investing intentionally
- making fewer random decisions
- noticing drift sooner
- linking actions to a longer-term plan
- accepting that capital may need to be reallocated over time

### Why they keep paying

Because the product keeps answering an ongoing question:

> “Am I getting where I want to go, and what should I change now to improve my odds?”

The subscription is justified as:

- much cheaper than a fund manager
- more structured than a spreadsheet
- more strategic than a broker app
- more empowering than handing over full control

## 10. The sharpest MVP statement

### One-line version

**A web app for active investors with multiple accounts that consolidates their portfolio, ties it to a long-term goal, and creates a weekly feedback loop to keep them on track.**

### Slightly richer version

**MarketState helps self-directed investors turn scattered accounts into a single goal-driven investing system by combining portfolio consolidation, realistic benchmarking, market context, and a recurring review loop that shows whether they are on track, why, and what to change.**

## 11. Recommended MVP v1 scope

If forced to cut aggressively, build **only this**:

1. onboarding with goal setup
2. simple assumptions / benchmark education
3. account import or manual portfolio entry
4. unified net worth / holdings dashboard
5. progress-to-goal tracker
6. benchmark comparison
7. weekly review prompt
8. holding-level drill-down
9. simple market and sector context

That is enough for a user to say:

> “This helps me invest with more clarity and control.”

---

## 12. What to postpone

Postpone these until after you validate retention:

- advanced research workflows
- heavy AI analysis
- full broker/instrument eligibility engine
- sophisticated journaling taxonomy
- social comparison features
- advisor marketplace
- automated trading integrations
- broad international complexity

---

## 13. Main product risks

### Risk 1: too broad
You could accidentally build half a broker, half a portfolio tracker, half an education app, and half a research tool.

**Response:** stay focused on the goal loop.

### Risk 2: users set up once and do not return
This becomes shelfware if there is no recurring ritual.

**Response:** design around the weekly and monthly review loop.

### Risk 3: too much education, not enough action
Users do not need a giant finance academy.

**Response:** education should only support goal-setting and decision quality.

### Risk 4: unclear differentiation
If it looks like “another dashboard,” it will be ignored.

**Response:** lead with control, progress, and course correction.

---

## 14. Best way forward

### Phase 1: validate manually
Before building deeply, test the concept with:

- landing page
- mockups
- 10–20 interviews
- manual spreadsheet/mock review flow
- paid early-access offer or lifetime deal for a small cohort

### Phase 2: build the narrow loop
Build the smallest product that delivers:

- clear goal
- full picture
- on-track signal
- weekly reflection

### Phase 3: expand only after retention
Only once users return consistently should you add:

- richer research
- better broker/instrument discovery
- sector/industry intelligence
- advanced optimization features

---

## 15. Final recommendation

The best first version of this business is:

**not** “an app to research and trade stocks”  
**not** “a generic net worth dashboard”  
**not** “an AI investing assistant”

It is:

**a goal-driven investing control center for active investors with multiple accounts, built around progress, reflection, and course correction.**
